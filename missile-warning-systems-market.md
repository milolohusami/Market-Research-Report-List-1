<p><p>Missile warning systems are essential for detecting incoming threats, and they can be categorized into three main types. Infrared-based systems utilize heat signatures to identify missiles during launch and flight. Pulse Doppler-based systems leverage radar technology to track fast-moving projectiles by analyzing frequency shifts. Ultraviolet-based systems detect the intense UV radiation emitted during a missile's propulsion. Each type offers distinct advantages in detection capabilities, contributing to enhanced situational awareness and defense strategies across military operations.</p></p>
